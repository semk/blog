In my downtime, I like to work on a handful of projects. Most of them are open-sourced and available on BitBucket or github. If you think any of these are fun, useful, entertaining, then cool. Feel free to drop me a line with comments or report any bugs to the appropriate bug-tracker.

mattaust.in

http://github.com/maafy6/mattaust.in

I'd had plans to make a small personal blog website for some time, and after I dumped the experiment I had been working with based on Hyde, I decided to give Jekyll (the project that originally inspired Hyde) a try. In addition to being a cozy campfire place for me to tell my stories, I am using it as a teaching tool for myself to learn Jekyll, git, and HTML5 and other web technologies.

lorem-ipsum

http://github.com/maafy6/lorem-ipsum

Started as a day project when I realized it would be handy to have some dummy content while working on my website. I knew I could just go to lipsum.org, but I thought it would be more fun to try and create a generator I could train. I still have some plans to expand it, but I did get it to a complete enough stage where I didn't feel ridiculous publishing it as a rubygem.

hg-config

http://bitbucket.org/maafy6/hg-config

Started when I wanted to begin familiarizing myself with some of the Mercurial source. Working with the configuration seemed like it would be something relatively safe and easy to test the waters. I had seen a number of questions on StackOverflow about why there wasn't a way to programmatically edit the configuration files à la git, so I began hacking away on this. I've since half come around to the reasoning as to why that functionality is not available, and accordingly the time I've spent on that extension has slowed accordingly.
